# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to OctoAcme's centralized repository for project management. This documentation outlines the repeatable processes, roles, and quality assurance practices designed to drive reliable and consistent outcomes across all projects.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management

OctoAcme follows a structured project management lifecycle covering initiation, planning, execution, release, and continuous improvement. Each stage relies on clearly defined artifacts and accountable roles—Product Managers own the vision and prioritization, Project Managers facilitate delivery and risk management, Developers execute and uphold quality, and QA ensures releases meet acceptance criteria. Team rhythms include daily standups, weekly syncs, and milestone retrospectives to maintain alignment and transparency.

Communication is prioritized through explicit cadence (standups, syncs, stakeholder updates), risk registers, and escalation paths. Processes are designed to surface risks early, with pathways for addressing issues at the team, product, and sponsor level. Decision rationale and deliverables (like the project one-pager, backlog, release notes, and retrospectives) are versioned here for easy access and ongoing reference.

Quality assurance is embedded at every stage. Code must pass automated CI checks (tests, lint, security), releases require full acceptance criteria and rollback plans, and all critical features are subject to manual QA. Continuous improvement is reinforced with regular retrospectives, tracked action items, and measurement of outcomes to adapt and refine processes over time.
